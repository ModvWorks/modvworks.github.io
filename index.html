About Me / Bio Sketch

👨‍💻

Frank Funny

I’m an Embedded & Control Systems Engineer focused on building real-time, industrial-grade intelligent systems. My work sits at the intersection of embedded hardware, real-time software, control theory, and physical systems. I don’t just design components—I architect complete systems where sensing, computation, and actuation come together reliably in the real world. With a background in electrical engineering and systems engineering (controls & automation), I approach problems from a systems-level perspective. Whether it's developing control algorithms, implementing sensor fusion, or deploying firmware on constrained hardware, I focus on solutions that are not only theoretically sound but also robust, efficient, and deployable. I’m particularly interested in autonomy and intelligent systems—where control meets decision-making. My work explores areas such as advanced control (LQR, MPC), real-time embedded systems, and AI-driven autonomy, especially in platforms like drones and industrial sensing systems. Beyond building, I believe in clarity. I break down complex engineering concepts into structured, understandable ideas—while actively building and sharing real systems in public. This is part of a long-term effort to develop meaningful intellectual property and contribute to the engineering community.

In short: I design, build, and explain systems that operate in the real world—reliably, intelligently, and in real time.

Plant Identification Tool
Upload time, input, output CSV — get G(s) back.

Upload a CSV of measured time-domain data (input/output pairs) and the tool estimates your plant's transfer function automatically. Fits first-order, second-order, and higher-order models using frequency-domain least-squares.
